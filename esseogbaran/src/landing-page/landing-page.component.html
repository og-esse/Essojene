About Me

I’m currently a Senior Software Engineer and creative technologist who loves problem solving. I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, intuitive interfaces. I get a lot of gratitude from seeing my work come to life in the browser and make an impact in the real world.

My journey started at Wilfrid Laurier University where I studied Computer Science and participated in a Hackathon. I worked on a connected glove that combined communication, warmth, and contactless payments—a project that won top prize at a fashion tech hackathon. Since then, I’ve brought that same mix of creativity and practicality into my real life work.

At RBC, I’ve shipped tools that directly impact thousands of users: from a digital mortgage renewal experience that boosted conversions, to an investments account flow that modernized a legacy journey, to design system work used across the bank to speed up development and improve consistency.

But what really keeps me going is system resilience. While I love delivering features, I get a different kind of thrill from understanding how systems behave under pressure. When something breaks in production, I’m usually the first one digging into logs, tracing requests across services, and asking “why” until the root cause is clear.

My toolkit includes Angular, React, Node.js, and GraphQL, alongside operational tools like Splunk, SQL, and Cypress. I work closely with product managers, security teams, and backend engineers to ship scalable, maintainable software rather than just one-off UI.

Outside of work, I run a creative brand called JN, where I explore identity and individuality through storytelling and technology. I’ve built multiple browser-based experiences from scratch, including JNGYM, Swag Shooter, and CYK2, where I experiment with interactive systems, custom rendering, and performance-driven design. If you’d like to see the code, I’m happy to share the repos on request.

I like learning new things in my free time. Last year I taught myself how to sew, DJ, and roller skate; this year it’s shaders and game engines. Whether it’s technology or life skills, I tend to go deep and stay curious.

Right now I’m looking for roles where I can combine engineering, investigation, and automation to solve critical problems.
